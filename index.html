Our Mission

We're a global environmental nonprofit working to create a world where people and nature can thrive together.

Founded in 1951 through grassroots action in the United States, The Nature Conservancy has grown to become one of the most effective and wide-reaching environmental organizations in the world. Our mission is to conserve the lands and waters on which all life depends.

We tackle the most pressing conservation challenges of our time: climate change, protecting lands and waters, providing food and water sustainably, and building healthy cities. Our approach is science-based and collaborative, working with local communities, governments, the private sector, and other partners to develop innovative, sustainable solutions.

With operations in 79 countries and territories, we employ more than 400 scientists and have protected more than 125 million acres of land and thousands of miles of rivers worldwide.

Our Programs

We address critical conservation challenges through focused initiatives that deliver measurable results.

Addressing the Climate Crisis

Climate change is the single biggest threat to our planet. We're working to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and help nature adapt to climate impacts through natural climate solutions.

Our approach focuses on protecting and restoring forests, improving agricultural practices, and conserving coastal wetlandsâ€”all of which can absorb and store significant amounts of carbon.

We also work with governments and businesses to implement policies and practices that reduce emissions and transition to clean energy.

Learn About Our Climate Work

Protecting Vital Lands

From forests to grasslands, we work to protect the most important lands for nature and people. Our science helps identify priority areas that provide critical habitat for wildlife while also delivering clean air, water, and other benefits to people.

We use a variety of conservation tools including land purchases, conservation easements, and partnerships with indigenous communities and local landowners.

To date, we've protected more than 125 million acres around the world, including iconic landscapes like the Amazon rainforest and the American West.

Explore Our Land Projects

Safeguarding Waters

Clean, abundant water is essential for all life. We work to protect freshwater ecosystems and ensure sustainable water supplies for people and nature.

Our water programs focus on protecting watersheds, restoring rivers and wetlands, and promoting sustainable water use in agriculture and cities.

We've helped conserve more than 5,000 miles of rivers and nearly 1 million acres of lakes and wetlands worldwide, while also improving water security for millions of people.

Discover Our Water Initiatives

Building Sustainable Cities

With more than half the world's population living in urban areas, cities play a crucial role in conservation. We work to integrate nature into urban planning and design.

Our urban conservation initiatives include creating green spaces, protecting urban watersheds, promoting green infrastructure, and connecting city dwellers with nature.

From rooftop gardens in New York to urban wetlands in China, we're helping make cities more livable and sustainable for people and wildlife alike.

Our Story

Founded in 1951, The Nature Conservancy is a global environmental nonprofit working to create a world where people and nature thrive. What began as a small group of dedicated scientists and conservationists in the United States has grown into one of the most effective and wide-reaching environmental organizations in the world.

Our journey started with the purchase of a single 60-acre parcel of land along the Mianus River Gorge in New York. This first project established our approach of using science to identify important natural areas and then taking direct action to protect them. Today, we operate in 79 countries and territories, employing more than 400 scientists and managing over 100 million acres of protected lands worldwide.

Our Mission

We conserve the lands and waters on which all life depends. Our vision is a world where the diversity of life thrives, and people act to conserve nature for its own sake and its ability to fulfill our needs and enrich our lives.

We achieve this through the dedicated efforts of our diverse staff, including more than 400 scientists, located in all 50 U.S. states and more than 35 countries across the world; with the help of our many partners, from individuals and governments to local nonprofits and corporations; and by using a non-confrontational, collaborative approach and staying true to our five unique core values.

Our Approach

The Nature Conservancy takes a science-based approach to conservation. We use the best available science to identify the most important lands and waters to protect, then develop innovative, practical solutions to the most pressing conservation challenges at scales that matter.

Our work is guided by five core values:

  • Integrity Beyond Reproach - We are committed to the highest standards of ethical behavior and accountability.
  • Respect for People, Communities, and Cultures - We recognize and honor the fundamental value and dignity of all individuals.
  • Commitment to Diversity - We are committed to a globally diverse and culturally competent workforce.
  • One Conservancy - We work together as one organization, leveraging our collective strengths.
  • Tangible, Lasting Results - We use a non-confrontational, collaborative approach and focus on measurable outcomes.

Our History

Key milestones in our conservation journey

1951

The Beginning

The Nature Conservancy is founded as a small nonprofit organization with the purchase of a 60-acre parcel along the Mianus River Gorge in New York.

1965

First International Project

We expand beyond U.S. borders with our first international project in Canada, protecting the Niagara Escarpment in Ontario.

1988

Debt-for-Nature Swap

We pioneer the debt-for-nature swap concept, converting foreign debt into conservation funding in Bolivia, setting a model for future international conservation finance.

2000

Global Expansion

The Conservancy launches major initiatives in Asia and the Pacific, establishing programs in China, Indonesia, and Micronesia.

2015

Climate Change Focus

We announce a major commitment to address climate change through natural solutions, pledging to protect and restore forests, wetlands, and grasslands that store carbon.

2020

30x30 Initiative

The Conservancy joins the global movement to protect 30% of the planet's land and water by 2030 to address biodiversity loss and climate change.

Our Commitment to Privacy

The Nature Conservancy ("we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting it through our compliance with this policy.
